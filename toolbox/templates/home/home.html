What is the VEDB?

The Visual Experience Database (VEDB) consists of over 717 sessions of individuals performing daily tasks. It includes world-view and eye-view videos captured with a custom headset, along with collected data. It aims to offer insight into human visual experience, providing a valuable resource for advancing research in visual perception. Applications include:

● Gaze tracking in head-fixed and head-free contexts

● Spatiotemporal image analysis

● Deep neural network improvements for scene and activity recognition

● Product development involving human-computer interaction

How do we fit in?

Admittedly, VEDB session data not particularly intuitive nor easy to understand; not everyone is a computer scientist, after all. In response, our team has developed an open-source Toolbox of Python applications which aim to provide an accessible and efficient tool for visualizing/analyzing a session's eye and head movement data. This component, the web app, offers:

● A user-friendly interface for synchronized video playback and data visualization

● A secure system for uploading sessions for later consumptions

● Python-based interactive graph generation
